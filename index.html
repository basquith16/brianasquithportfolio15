The Iron Yard

Welcome to my website! If you've frequented my page, you have probably noticed that the design has completely changed three or four times and it's only been a month. Am I that fickle? Do I get bored easily? The answer to those two questions is a resounding yes, but that is not why the site keeps changing.

There are a lot of reasons why I decided to become a software engineer. At the top of that list is the reality that every second there is a new technology, program, or technique to learn. The industry is in a constant state of flux, which means you would have to try extremely hard to become bored. There is always something to learn and implement.

So where did this ability to research various technologies, study them, and then use them, come from? I am so glad that you asked that question.

The Iron Yard is known as a 'coding bootcamp', where students are immersed in an area of computer programming in which they are interested. For example, I chose to be in the front end development course, where I learned about Javascript, HTML, CSS, various frameworks, and other helpful tools. What you may not hear about the Iron Yard is how it is so much more than 'learning code.'

The philosophy of instruction is to put you at a healthy level of stress, loading your brain with information, and triggering the fight or flight instinct we are each born with. This sharpens your brain, increases focus, and maximizes your potential to learn. Equally important, they teach you how to think like a programmer and they mimic a software development work environment. This way you are equipped to solve problems as they arise in your own creative ways. Like the old proverb states, "Give a man a fish and you feed him for a day; teach a man to fish and you feed him for a lifetime." The Iron Yard does not feed its students code and frameworks. The Iron Yard teaches you how to learn coding languages, frameworks, and many other aspects of software development.
